# Occult Liminal Backrooms Track Brief

## Working Title

**There Was Never a Door**

## Core Thesis

This track is not about a monster. It is about realizing that the room itself is older than memory, and that memory is being used as the instrument.

The piece should feel like a decayed ballroom record discovered in a sealed government basement under an abandoned office complex. At first it is almost nostalgic: a distant old melody, a woman singing somewhere through a wall, shellac warmth, slow tape movement. Then the listener realizes the song is not being played inside a room. The song is the room.

Every loop should feel like an architectural mistake. Every pitch drift should feel like reality losing calibration. Every vocal smear should feel like a human presence trying to form, failing, and becoming part of the fluorescent hum.

The emotional arc is:

```text
familiarity -> wrongness -> disorientation -> ritualized clinical fear -> memory collapse -> fluorescent nothing
```

This must not become breakcore, EDM horror, trailer music, or goofy percussion. The fear should come from realism, depth, melody corruption, cursed human presence, unstable spatial perspective, and slow psychological pressure.

## Sonic Identity

Use one beautiful, old, public-domain ballroom, torch-song, or vocal fragment as the musical heart. Prefer a female vocal or choir-adjacent recording with a memorable melodic contour. The hook should be real and almost comforting before it is damaged.

The listener should be able to hum the ghost of the melody, but never hear it cleanly for long.

The melody returns in corrupted forms:

1. Distant, slowed, and mostly recognizable.
2. Slightly late, detuned downward, and wider than it should be.
3. Missing notes, with reversed tails arriving before the notes.
4. Stretched into vowel-like choir shadows.
5. Barely present, as if the room remembers the melody after the person is gone.

## Required Palette

- Old public-domain ballroom or vocal sample, slowed and detuned.
- High female singing fragments, stretched and formant-shifted.
- Reversed inhale swells and breath-like vocal ghosts.
- Whisper textures with no intelligible words.
- Fluorescent ballast hum and dead-office electrical tone.
- HVAC drones and concrete room resonance.
- Distant elevator-cable groans.
- Record crackle only when tied to the source record, not pasted-on static.
- Tape wow, flutter, dropouts, warble, and reel instability.
- Sparse low thuds every 13-19 seconds, never on-grid.
- Bowed metal, scraping pipes, and far impacts.
- Low sub pressure that is felt more than heard.
- Impossible-room reverb: tiny closet, endless hallway, tiled pool, dead office, basement archive.

## Hard Avoid List

- Breakcore.
- Fast drums.
- Bongos or hand percussion.
- Trap drums.
- EDM drops.
- Bright clean pads.
- Cheesy synth leads.
- Stock horror stingers.
- Intelligible subliminal speech or commands.
- Clean modern vocal chops.
- Static generators that sound detached from the record.
- Preset horror loops.

## Arrangement

Target duration: **4:13**.

### 0:00-0:38 - The Memory Still Works

A distant old song plays as if through a wall. The vocal or ballroom phrase is audible but slowed. Keep it narrow, warm, and almost mono. Add source-native record crackle only. The room feels small and plausible.

### 0:38-1:10 - The Fall

Pitch begins to sink. The stereo image bends left, then behind the head. Reverb expands from a closet into an endless hallway. The melody repeats slightly too late. A reversed vocal inhale pulls the listener downward.

### 1:10-1:52 - Arrival Under Fluorescent Light

The ballroom sample becomes a damaged loop. Add fluorescent hum, HVAC tone, and concrete resonance. Low sub pressure enters slowly. The melody remains, but notes are missing. The first distant building thud appears, not in rhythm.

### 1:52-2:42 - The Experiment Room

Clinical tape artifacts enter: relay clicks, reel start/stop, old lab-machine tones. Add abstract numbers-station atmosphere, but no clear numbers or commands. The vocal becomes breath and formant ghosts. Begin the salvia loop: a short motif repeats at uneven lengths, folding over itself until it feels like a stuck thought.

### 2:42-3:34 - Occult Collapse

The old song becomes almost choral. Stretch the female vocal into cursed harmonies. Reverse tails appear before notes. The room size changes mid-note: tiled pool, office, hallway, basement. Sub pressure grows, then suddenly drops out.

### 3:34-4:13 - No Exit

Remove most low end for about 10 seconds. Leave fluorescent hum, tape instability, and a nearly silent vocal smear. Then bring back one huge controlled sub swell under the final ghost of the melody. End on hum and degraded tape, not a clean resolution.

## Layer Roles

Every layer must have a job.

| Layer | Role |
| --- | --- |
| Melody | Decayed memory and emotional hook. |
| Vocal ghosts | Human presence dissolving into the room. |
| Fluorescent hum | The Backrooms as a physical place. |
| Sub pressure | Panic below consciousness. |
| Thuds and groans | The building remembering something. |
| Tape damage | Time and memory failing. |
| Reverb automation | Impossible architecture moving around the listener. |

If a sound does not deepen the place, remove it.

## Mix Thesis

The mix should be dark, wide, realistic, and controlled. It should feel cinematic and expensive, not noisy. Keep headroom. Avoid harsh digital clipping. The low end must be huge but disciplined. Crackle should feel sourced from old media, not like white noise pasted on top.

The final master should feel like a recovered tape that should not exist: beautiful enough to pull the listener in, wrong enough to make them regret listening.

Suggested master target:

```text
Peak ceiling: -1.0 dBTP
Integrated loudness: -16 to -13 LUFS
Low-end focus: controlled sub below 80 Hz
Stereo: wide ambience, stable low-end mono compatibility
Noise: source-native texture only, no detached hiss layer
```
